Nawabon ka Shahar-Lucknow

Lucknow is the capital city of Uttar Pradesh and it has always been a multicultural city. Lucknow has always been a multicultural city that flourished as a North Indian cultural and artistic hub, and the seat of power of Nawabs in the 18th and 19th centuries. It continues to be an important centre of governance, administration, education, commerce, aerospace, finance, pharmaceuticals, technology, design, culture, tourism, music and poetry.

Bara Imambara

Bara Imambara, also known as Asfi Imambara is an imambara complex in Lucknow, India built by Asaf-ud-Daula, Nawab of Awadh in 1784.

Timings: Sunrise to Sunset; every day Entry Fee: ₹ 25 for Indians; ₹ 500 for foreign nationals

Rumi Darwaza

This gateway built in 1784 by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula is about 60-feet tall and is generally used as the logo for Lucknow city.

Timings: 24×7 Entry Fee: Free

Husainabad Clock Tower

Located adjacent to the Rumi Darwaza, Husainabad Clock Tower is another heritage monument dotting the cityscape of Lucknow.

Timings: 24×7; every day Entry Fee: Free

Amrapali Water Park

When you are in the mood for some aquatic fun, head off to Amrapali Water Park, one of the best water parks in Lucknow.

Timings: 10:00 am to 6:00 pm; every day Entry Fee: ₹ 800 for adults; ₹ 600 for kids

Aminabad Market

One of the best places to shop in Lucknow is the Aminabad Market, which has been in operation since the times of the Nawabs.

Timings: Morning till night; closed on Thursdays Must-visit destinations: Madan Sarees, Sargodha Cloth House, Keshav Décor, Om Prakash Seth, Dupatta Mahal, and Sri Keshav Handloom

Hazratganj Market

The Hazratganj Market, a century-old shopping area located in the heart of Lucknow, is where you can find the city’s vibrant soul

Timings: Morning till night; closed on Sundays Must-visit destinations: Sahara Mall, Janpath, Kashmir Government Arts Emporium, Gandhi Ashram, Ram Advani Booksellers, Naza Computer Market

Indira Gandhi Planetarium

A visit to the Indira Gandhi Planetarium must be on your list of things to do in Lucknow, especially if you are a science enthusiast or traveling with your kids

Timings: 10:00 am to 5:00 pm; closed on Mondays Entry Fee: ₹ 25 per person

Ambedkar Park

A public park and memorial built across 107 acres in Gomti Nagar, Ambedkar Park honors Dr. Ambedkar, Jyotirao Phule, Kanshi Ram, Narayana Guru, and many other social reformers who worked for equality and social justice.

Timings: 11:00 am to 9:00 pm; every day Entry Fee: ₹ 15 per person
